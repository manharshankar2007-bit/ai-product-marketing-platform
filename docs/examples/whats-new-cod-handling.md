Subject: 🔥⚡️ What's New: COD Handling Logics for Riders

*Smart COD: Secure, Automated, and Ready!*

Managing Cash on Delivery (COD) comes with operational risk — from assigning the right riders to controlling how much cash is held in the field. With this release, we're introducing a **comprehensive COD management system** that gives you full control over **who handles cash, how much they carry, and what happens when limits are breached** — now with **more flexibility in order allocation control**.

**What's New**

1. **Controlled COD Allocation (Eligibility-Based):** Define which riders are eligible to handle COD orders using criteria like Lifetime completed orders, Orders in the last 7 days, and Average daily performance. Riders who don't qualify will not receive COD orders and will continue receiving prepaid orders.

2. **Cash Limits with Configurable Enforcement:** Set limits on how much cash a rider can hold — Max cash outstanding (cash in hand + newly allocated orders), Excess over daily earnings, and Excess over weekly earnings (both enabled with the Earnings & Incentives module). Limits apply considering new orders will also be allocated — a single order or all orders in a route are checked to ensure limits are met.

3. **Rider Breaching Defined Limits:** You now have flexibility in how strictly you want to enforce restrictions — Block All Order Allocation (COD + Prepaid), Block Only COD Order Allocation, or Take No Action initially and monitor patterns to enforce cleaner restrictions later.

4. **Smart Alerts & Threshold Monitoring:** The new "Cash Exceeded Threshold" alert auto-triggers when limits are breached, linked directly to configured cash thresholds, and supports automated actions for faster resolution — Logout Rider from Shift, or Assign Admin Task to return to hub and deposit cash.

**What This Means To You**

1. Assign COD orders only to qualified riders.
2. Control how much cash is in the field at any time.
3. Choose between strict blocking or partial restriction based on your operations.
4. Take no immediate action for rider operations but get notified in real-time to observe rider behaviour.
5. Automate enforcement and reduce manual monitoring.
6. Improve cash reconciliation and reduce leakage risk.
